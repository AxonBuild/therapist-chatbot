# Clinical Case Document

## Demographics
Female, 41 years old

## Summary of Issues
- Sleep disorder with frequent night awakenings (4 to 6 times per night) and difficulty falling back asleep
- Latent nocturnal hypervigilance triggered by minor noises or intrusive thoughts
- Anxious anticipation of awakening, creating a cycle of sleep-related anxiety
- High mental load with rumination about the next day’s tasks during the night
- Chronic daytime fatigue, irritability, lack of concentration, and guilt
- Background of a family environment where “rest was not valued,” with an ingrained belief: “I must always be in control or things will fall apart”

## Dominant Emotions
- Anticipatory anxiety
- Frustration and helplessness
- Guilt about not being “productive” during the day
- Feeling of losing control

## Triggers and Mechanisms
- Phase 1: Light sleep onset with body relaxed but mind on alert
- Phase 2: Sudden awakening after 90–120 minutes
- Phase 3: Automatic mental scanning for noises or forgotten tasks
- Phase 4: Cognitive activation with intrusive thoughts and projections about the next day
- Phase 5: Struggling against wakefulness, reinforcing insomnia
- Childhood association of vigilance with safety due to an unstable or anxious environment

## Limiting Beliefs
- If I sleep deeply, I might miss something important.
- I always need to be ready to respond, even at night.
- I’m weak if I rest too much.

## Proposed Solutions
### Immediate Solutions
- “4-7-8” breathing exercise upon awakening to soothe the nervous system
- Nighttime grounding card with comforting thoughts or mantra
- Get out of bed after 20 minutes of active wakefulness

### Intermediate Solutions
- Sleep reconditioning through sensory anchoring
- Night awakening journal with kind, pressure-free self-observation
- Belief retraining via cognitive restructuring

### Long-Term Solutions
- Regular hypnosis to deactivate internal alert systems
- Work on inner safety to reinforce the idea that night is a space for release
- Evening closure rituals

### Recommended Personalized Hypnosis Session
- Title: “Let the Night Watch Over Me”
- Objective: Deactivate nocturnal hypervigilance, instill safety, and create an inner guardian
- Duration: 18–20 minutes
- Format: Slow, reassuring voice with ambiance of soft rain or crackling fire
- Structure: Progressive induction, metaphor of a benevolent watcher, final anchor

## Progress Indicators
- Week 1: Track frequency and intensity of awakenings
- Week 2: Integrate sensory anchor and evaluate nighttime anxiety level
- Week 3: Signs of stabilization in sleep phases, beginning of rest revaluation
- Week 4: Reduced awakenings (1–2 max), noticeable evening calmness
